Assistant Professor

I am an Assistant Professor at the University of South Florida. My research focuses on AI security, adversarial machine learning, trustworthy biometrics, privacy-preserving machine learning, hardware security, and secure systems for real-world deployment.

My group works at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, biometrics, and hardware-rooted trust, with recent projects on adversarial diffusion models, cancelable biometric key generation, synthetic contactless fingerphoto generation, and robust AI systems.

Research

My research develops secure, trustworthy, and privacy-aware AI systems. I am particularly interested in adversarial learning, generative AI security, biometric template protection, cancelable biometrics, synthetic biometric generation, hardware security, and AI-enabled anomaly detection.

AI Security

Adversarial attacks, diffusion-based protection, backdoors, poisoning, and robust model evaluation.

Biometric Security

Cancelable templates, biometric key generation, face privacy, contactless fingerprints, and anti-spoofing.

Hardware Security

Root of trust, secure boot, chiplet security, authentication, and trusted systems.

AI for Inspection

Machine learning for anomaly detection in X-ray cargo inspection and safety-critical sensing.
